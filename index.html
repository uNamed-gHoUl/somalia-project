Somalia Overview

Somalia is in East Africa and the capital is Mogadishu. The population is an estimated 14,650,000. The offical currency is the Somali Shilling.

Culture and People of Somalia

  • The offical languages are Somali and Arabic.
  • To say "Hello," people in Somalia say: Sidee tahay?
  • The most popular religion is Islam.
  • Football is popular.
  • The most commonly celebrated holiday is New Year's Day, which is celebrated using firework displays at 00:00.
  • Buses, minibuses, and trucks are used for transportation.
  • Meats like goat, beef, lamb and chicken are staples, as well as rice and noodles. Sambusas are deep-fried, rectangular dumplings filled with meat and/or vegetables, and anjara is a large, spongy, pancake-like homemade bread.
  • Most people live in either an African roundhouse called a mundul or an Arab-influenced rectangular house called a cariish.
  • Somalia is in the midst of a civil war. A drought is also going on in Somalia and famine is going on in the lower part of the country.
  • Somalia became independent in 1960.
  • I recommend visiting the National Museum of Somalia because it tells a lot about the history of Somalia and displays some of its most valuable artifacts.

Nature and Geography

  • Some animals that inhabit Somalia are lions, giraffes, cheetahs, baboons, and elephants.
  • Geographic features include plateaus, plains, highlands, the Jubba river and the Shabeelle river.
  • To the north of my country is Yemen.
  • To the south of my country are Kenya and the Indian Ocean.
  • To the east of my country are Kenya and Ethiopia.
  • To the west of my country is the Indian Ocean.
  • Climate is arid to semiarid, has two annual rainfall seasons.

Fun Facts

  1. Somalia has the longest coastline in mainland Africa
  2. Has one of the lowest life expectancies in the world
  3. The official name is the Federal Republic of Somalia
  4. 637,657 square kilometres large
  5. When dining at home, it is customary for the women to serve the men first
  6. The Civil War in Somalia has been going on for over a decade
  7. Somalia is one of the poorest countries in the world
  8. More than half of Somalians are self-employed
  9. Somalia was formed on July 1st, 1990
  10. Over 73% of Somalians live on less than 2 US dollars per day
